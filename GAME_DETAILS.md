# FORCE PROJECTION: JOINT COMMAND (Reforged Edition)

> A competitive strategy game about procurement, basing, alliances, and global posture — built around a Budget Cycle + Program Portfolio system, not a "Terraforming-style" action economy.

This version changes the core loop so it's not "play cards until everyone passes, then produce." Instead you run a Portfolio, commit to Quarterly Orders, and fight over Congressional Agendas + Contract Obligations that reshape the whole table every year.

---

## 1. What You're Doing

You are a **Joint Command director**. Across multiple Fiscal Years, you:

- Build a portfolio of programs (F-22/F-35 squadrons, carrier groups, expeditionary units, ISR networks)
- Expand bases, alliances, and forward ops on a global theater board
- Win Congressional support to pass agendas that benefit you (or hurt rivals)
- Take Contracts (public objectives with obligations) for points and perks
- Keep the national posture strong enough to avoid blowback when crises hit

You're competing for **Strategic Influence (SI)**. Highest SI wins.

---

## 2. What Makes This Version Fundamentally Different

### A) The game runs in QUARTERS with simultaneous planning

Each Fiscal Year has 4 Quarters. In each Quarter, players choose 2 Orders simultaneously and reveal them. Orders resolve in a specific sequence that creates interaction and counterplay.

### B) You manage a limited PROGRAM PORTFOLIO

You don't just stack cards endlessly. Each player board has:

- **Program Slots** (Active programs)
- **Pipeline Slots** (programs being developed; they "mature" later)
- **Mothballed** (inactive programs you can reactivate)

This creates hard choices, timing windows, and prevents runaway "tableau sprawl."

### C) Budgets are segmented into budget lines

Budget isn't one generic money pile. You generate and spend:

| Code | Budget Line |
|------|-------------|
| **A** | AIR Budget |
| **S** | SEA Budget |
| **E** | EXP Budget (expeditionary/Marine) |
| **X** | SPACE/CYBER Budget |
| **U** | SUSTAIN Budget (maintenance/logistics/readiness) |

You can reprogram between lines, but it costs **Political Capital (PC)**.

### D) Congressional Agendas + Contracts drive the year

Every Fiscal Year begins with a public Agenda and a public Contract Market. These create shared "what matters this year" pressure and change game flow.

- Budget tokens in 5 colors (A/S/E/X/U)

## 4. Key Concepts & Stats

### Strategic Influence (SI)

Your victory points. You gain SI from:

- Completed Contracts
- Theater Control
- Agendas you helped pass + benefited from
- Program VP (some cards have printed SI)
- Endgame sets (tags, portfolio quality, stability)

### National Posture (shared pressure, not a point track)

Instead of global parameters that everyone farms for score, this edition tracks National Posture as three shared thresholds that affect crises and scoring:

| Threshold | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Coverage** | How many theaters have meaningful U.S. presence |
| **Readiness** | Table-wide ability to avoid backlash |
| **Tech Edge** | Enables certain high-end programs/contracts |

These are not straight points; they're **gates and risk modifiers**.

### Theater Strength (for control)

In each theater, each player has Strength from:

| Source | Strength |
|--------|----------|
| Base | 2 |
| Alliance | 1 |
| Forward Ops | 3 |
| Stationed Programs | +1 or +2 (varies) |

## 5. Setup (2-5 players)

1. Place the Global Theater Board center.
2. Shuffle Agenda, Contract, Crisis, and Program decks.
3. Deal each player:
   - **Starting tokens** (recommended baseline):
     - A=2, S=2, E=2, X=2, U=2
     - M=2, L=2, I=1, PC=1
     - SI=0
   - **Starting production**:
     - A/S/E/X/U production = 1 each
     - M/L production = 1 each
     - I production = 0
     - PC production = 0
4. Each player chooses a **Directorate** (see section 6).
5. Each player draws 8 Program cards, keeps 6, discards 2.
6. Reveal 1 Agenda (Year 1 Agenda).
7. Reveal 3 Contracts (Contract Market).
8. Reveal 1 Crisis and resolve "Start of Year" text (if any).

---

## 6. Directorates (Factions)

These are asymmetrical but bounded by the Portfolio + Budget-line constraints.

### NAVSEA -- Fleet & Shipyard Command

- **Passive:** When you place a SEA-tag Program into Active, gain +1 U (Sustain) immediately.
- **Once per Year:** Reprogram 1 budget between lines for free.
- **Start bonus:** +1 S production, +1 U production.

### AIRCOM -- Air Dominance Command

- **Passive:** The first AIR-tag Program you activate each Year costs 1 less A and gives +1 I.
- **Once per Year:** If you win a Theater Control tie, you win it outright.
- **Start bonus:** +1 A production, +1 M.

### MARFOR -- Expeditionary Command

- **Passive:** Your Forward Ops placements cost -1 E.
- **Once per Year:** When you complete a Contract, gain +1 PC.
- **Start bonus:** +1 E production, +1 L.

### SPACECY -- Space & Cyber Command

- **Passive:** Your first NETWORK program you activate each Year gives +1 I production.
- **Once per Year:** Peek at the next Crisis; you may pay 1 PC to bury it and draw a new one.
- **Start bonus:** +1 X production, +1 I.

### TRANSCOM -- Mobility & Sustainment Command

- **Passive:** Any time you pay L, pay 1 less L (min 0).
- **Once per Year:** Convert 2 U -> 1 of any line without PC cost.
- **Start bonus:** +1 U production, +1 L production.

## 7. Card Types & the Portfolio System

### Program Cards

Most programs are not played directly into a sprawling tableau. You must choose:

- **Pipeline** (face-up in Pipeline slot): cheaper now, but delayed payoff
- **Active** (goes into an Active slot): full effect now, but uses scarce slots

### Program States

| State | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Pipeline** | You may invest over time; it "matures" at year end |
| **Active** | Contributes effects, tags, and sometimes theater strength |
| **Mothballed** | You can slide an Active program here to free a slot; reactivation costs |

This is the main "anti-ripoff" backbone: the game is about **portfolio timing and slot pressure**, not raw card accumulation.

## 8. The Year Structure (Core Turn Loop)

Each Fiscal Year has four phases:

### Phase A -- Congress (Agenda Vote)

Reveal 1 Agenda (public). Players secretly choose how much PC they commit to:

- **Support** or **Oppose** the Agenda.
- Reveal simultaneously.
- If Support > Oppose: **Agenda passes**
- Else: **Agenda fails** (often still has partial effects)

The Agenda applies for the entire Year and typically:

- Buffs certain theaters
- Discounts certain program types
- Imposes restrictions (e.g., "reprogramming costs +1 PC")
- Changes Contract rewards

### Phase B -- Contract Market

1. Reveal Contracts until 3 are available.
2. In turn order, each player may take one Contract (max 2 active Contracts per player).

Contracts give:

- An immediate perk (budget, production, placement)
- An obligation to satisfy by year end (or you take a penalty)
- SI if completed

### Phase C -- Four Quarters (the heart of the game)

Each Quarter has:

#### 1) Crisis Pulse

Reveal a Crisis for that Quarter. It has:

- An immediate twist (cost increases, theater demand, etc.)
- A Quarter Test (optional) that rewards response

#### 2) Plan Orders (Simultaneous)

Each player chooses 2 Orders from their Order list, places them face down, then reveal.

#### 3) Resolve Orders (in sequence)

Orders resolve in this order:

1. **Influence** (political, contracts, agenda manipulation)
2. **Procure** (start or activate programs)
3. **Deploy** (bases/alliances/forward ops, stationing)
4. **Sustain** (readiness, logistics, mitigating crisis)

If multiple players chose the same Order, resolve in initiative order for that step.

#### 4) End of Quarter Cleanup

- Contract checks that say "by end of Quarter"
- Discard down to hand limit (default 7)
- **Program draw:** each player draws from the program deck **only** after **Q1** and **Q3** cleanup (i.e. when entering Q2 and Q4). No program draw after Q2 cleanup or after Q4 (year end).
- **Hand limit:** if you are above the hand limit (default 7), you **choose** which programs to discard to the discard pile before the quarter advances (not automatic).

### Phase D -- Year End

- **Portfolio Maturation:** each Pipeline program either:
  - Moves to Active (if you have a free Active slot and pay maturation cost), or
  - Remains in Pipeline (pay a small upkeep), or
  - Is discarded
- **Contract Completion:** score and penalties
- **Theater Control Scoring** (optional each year, always at endgame)
- **Upkeep & Readiness Check**

## 9. Orders (Your Main Actions)

Each Quarter you pick **2**.

### Influence Orders

#### A) Lobby

Gain +2 PC. If Agenda passed, also gain +1 of the Agenda's favored budget line.

#### B) Negotiate

Place 1 Alliance in a theater where you have a Base, or spend 1 PC to place it anywhere.

#### C) Contracting

Draw 2 Contracts, keep 1 (put the other on bottom). If you already have 2 active Contracts, instead gain +1 SI.

### Procure Orders

#### D) Start Program (Pipeline)

Choose a Program from hand and place it into a Pipeline slot by paying its Pipeline cost.

#### E) Activate Program (Active)

Pay full cost, move a Program directly into an Active slot and gain its immediate effect.

#### F) Refit / Mothball

Move 1 Active program to Mothball (gain +1 U), or reactivate 1 Mothballed program (pay 1 U + 1 of its budget line).

### Deploy Orders

#### G) Build Base

Pay 2 U + 1 of any line; place a Base in any theater with an open Base slot.

#### H) Forward Ops

**Requirement:** you have a Base in that theater. Pay 1 L + 2 U; place Forward Ops in that theater's Forward slot.

#### I) Station Programs

Move up to 2 Stationed markers from your Active Programs to theaters (if the program allows), gaining theater strength and sometimes Contract progress.

### Sustain Orders

#### J) Major Exercise

Pay 1 M + 1 U; gain Readiness +2 (your personal readiness), and draw 1 card.

#### K) Logistics Surge

Gain +2 L and reduce the Quarter Crisis penalty against you by 1.

#### L) Intel Focus

Gain +2 I. If you spend 1 I, you may change your revealed Order resolution position by +1 (tactical initiative trick).

---

## 10. Resources & Reprogramming

### Paying Costs

Costs specify budget lines and/or M/L/I/PC.

### Reprogramming (Moving budget between lines)

At any time during Procure resolution:

- Pay **1 PC** to convert 1 budget token from one line to another.
- Some Agendas make this cheaper/harder.

This creates a very different economy from "one money pool."

## 12. Contracts (The Other Big Differentiator)

Contracts are public missions with obligations. Each Contract has:

- **Immediate Award** (right now)
- **Requirements** (things to do this year)
- **Completion SI**
- **Failure penalty**

### Example Contracts

#### Air Dominance Demonstration

| | |
|---|---|
| **Take** | Gain +2A immediately |
| **Complete** | Station 1 Fighter program in Indo-Pacific and have Readiness >= 4 |
| **Reward** | +5 SI |
| **Fail** | Lose 2 SI and discard 1 card |

#### Sustainment Surge

| | |
|---|---|
| **Take** | +2U |
| **Complete** | Perform Sustain Orders in 3 different Quarters |
| **Reward** | +4 SI, +1 U production |
| **Fail** | -1 U production for next year |

#### Alliance Architecture

| | |
|---|---|
| **Take** | Place 1 Alliance immediately |
| **Complete** | 3 Alliances across 3 theaters |
| **Reward** | +6 SI |
| **Fail** | Lose 1 Alliance of your choice |

#### Polar Coverage

| | |
|---|---|
| **Take** | +1X, +1U |
| **Complete** | Base in Arctic and Forward Ops in Arctic or N. Atlantic |
| **Reward** | +5 SI |
| **Fail** | -2 SI |

Contracts create strong yearly arcs and interaction that TM-style engine games often lack.

## 13. Crises

Crises hit **per Quarter**, not once per year as a simple global modifier.

Crisis cards include:

- Immediate table rule
- Response window (specific Orders help)
- Penalty if ignored

### Example Crisis: Budget Freeze

| | |
|---|---|
| **This Quarter** | Activate Program costs +1 U |
| **Response** | Any player who chose Lobby may ignore the +1 U |
| **Penalty** | If fewer than 2 players performed a Sustain Order this Quarter, everyone loses 1 SI |

---

## 14. Theater Control (Interaction Without "Combat")

### Strength Calculation

> Base (2) + Alliance (1) + Forward Ops (3) + Stationed Program bonuses

### When It Scores

You can score Theater Control at **Year End** (recommended) and at **Endgame**.

| Placement | SI Reward |
|-----------|-----------|
| 1st place | 4 SI |
| 2nd place | 2 SI |
| 3rd place (4-5 players only) | 1 SI |

This keeps pressure on the map but avoids elimination/attack mechanics.

---

## 15. Hand, Draw, and Deck Flow

- **Default hand limit:** 7
- **You draw:**
  - 2 Program cards after **Q1** and **Q3** cleanup (when entering Q2 and Q4)
  - Plus effects from Orders/programs
- Discard freely only when an effect says so; otherwise discard at end of Quarter down to limit.

---

## 16. Endgame & Winning

### Game Length

| Variant | Duration |
|---------|----------|
| Standard | 4 Fiscal Years (16 Quarters) |
| Shorter | 3 Years |
| Longer | 5 Years |

### Final Scoring

- Total SI
- **Endgame Program set bonuses:**
  - +3 SI for each full set of (Air, Sea, Exp, Space/Cyber) tags in Active (max 2 sets)
  - +2 SI if you completed all Contracts you took (no failures)
  - +1 SI per theater where you have any presence (Base/Alliance/Forward Ops)

### Tiebreakers

1. Most completed Contracts
2. Most theaters controlled at endgame
3. Most PC remaining

---

## 17. Balance Notes

This edition self-balances through:

- **Portfolio slot limits** -- prevents infinite stacking
- **Budget lines** -- prevents perfect efficiency
- **Quarterly order reveal** -- counterplay & timing
- **Contracts** -- points come from commitments, not passive engine growth

## 18. Optional Variants

### Drafted Contract Market

Instead of open 3, reveal 5; in reverse order, each player takes 1; discard the rest.

### Fog of Crisis

Reveal only the Crisis title; full text flips after Orders are chosen.

### Directorate Lock-in

Your directorate grants one "signature" starting program in Pipeline (different for each faction).
